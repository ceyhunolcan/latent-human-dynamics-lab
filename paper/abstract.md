# Abstract

Passive sensing produces dense, longitudinal, multimodal records of human physiology, behavior, and environmental exposure. Most modeling work on these data collapses the time series into a single regression onto a self-report outcome, which throws away the temporal structure of the underlying biology and leaves nowhere good to do counterfactual reasoning.

This work proposes a multimodal latent state-space framework instead. The framework learns a low-dimensional, physiologically interpretable manifold from wearable, behavioral, and climate streams, fits an explicit transition model on the manifold, and exposes a perturbation operator that simulates how the trajectory would have evolved under counterfactual interventions: extended sleep, behavioral change, cooling, air-quality improvement, heat-wave shock.

The six-dimensional latent state has named axes (autonomic recovery, circadian alignment, stress load, environmental burden, behavioral instability, missingness pressure) and is encoded by a modality-specific encoder regularized for smoothness, next-state predictability, and disentanglement. Missingness is treated as a state-dependent feature rather than nuisance, because in passive sensing dropout co-varies with the same phenomena the model is trying to recover. Environmental forcing enters the dynamics through an Environmental Physiological Load (EPL) composite of daytime heat, nighttime heat, air quality, and consecutive heat-wave exposure, scaled by a per-participant vulnerability coefficient that can be fit or held fixed.

The release ships a complete research stack on a synthetic cohort calibrated to plausible human ranges: feature engineering, latent encoding, regime detection, energy-landscape estimation, neural and classical dynamics, counterfactual simulation, robustness and subgroup evaluation, and a FastAPI service. The framework is non-clinical: it is a modeling and simulation tool for researchers, not a diagnostic or treatment device. The discussion lays out what it would take to bring the framework to external validation in real cohorts, and proposes a falsifiable path via identifiability analysis, hierarchical Bayesian personalization of resilience, and pre-registered out-of-distribution evaluation.
