# Limitations

These are named explicitly so downstream users don't have to reverse-engineer them from the code.

## Synthetic-data scope

Every quantitative claim in the released artifacts is computed on synthetic data generated by a model whose structure is known to its authors. The encoder recovers latent axes that the generator put in. The regime detector finds clusters the generator labeled. The perturbation operator produces effect sizes that the generator's coefficients permit. None of this licenses claims about real human dynamics. Treat the released numbers as demonstrations of capability, not effect estimates.

## Identifiability of the dynamics drift

The drift $f_\theta(z, E, B, P)$ is implemented as a sum of contraction, linear environmental forcing, linear behavioral forcing, and an additive perturbation. With six latent dimensions, four environmental channels, four behavioral channels, and a structured perturbation operator, several distinct parameterizations of $f_\theta$ can produce indistinguishable trajectories on finite cohorts. Identifiability has not been formally established. A proper treatment would characterize the equivalence classes of $(\kappa, \alpha_E, \alpha_B, v)$ that are observationally equivalent under realistic observation noise, report posterior intervals on each coefficient under a hierarchical prior, and provide a sensitivity analysis to encoder regularization strength.

## Causal interpretation

Counterfactual trajectories are produced under a model. The perturbation operator encodes a specific causal assumption: that the intervention acts additively on the latent state with a specific direction vector and decay. That assumption is not testable from the released synthetic cohort because the cohort was generated under the same assumption. In real cohorts the assumption can fail in any of several ways. The intervention may also change the participant's vulnerability coefficient (path coupling). The response may be non-linear in magnitude. The perturbation may interact with the environmental forcing rather than adding to it. Treat outputs as simulated trajectories, not effect estimates.

## Calibration of environmental forcing

The Environmental Physiological Load (EPL) weights are literature-derived defaults and they are uniform across the cohort. In reality the relative weights of daytime heat, nighttime heat, AQI, and consecutive heat-wave days depend on housing quality, age, comorbidity profile, and acclimatization. The framework exposes a `vulnerability_coefficient` scalar to absorb some of this variation. It is a placeholder rather than a derived quantity.

## Missingness model

Missingness is treated as state-dependent, which is a strict improvement over treating it as missing-at-random. But the actual missingness mechanism in real passive-sensing cohorts is multi-causal: device fault, charging behavior, social context, study fatigue, and yes, physiological state. The released framework lumps these into a single state-dependent dropout probability. A real-cohort version should at minimum separate device-induced missingness from behavioral withdrawal.

## Subgroup coverage

The synthetic generator samples baselines independently, which over-represents healthy normo-resilient phenotypes relative to the populations one most wants this work to serve. Subgroup performance numbers on the synthetic cohort are therefore optimistic for the easy subgroups and uninformative for the hardest ones. Real-cohort evaluation should stratify by climate-vulnerability tertile, by missingness tertile, and by self-reported chronic-condition status, and should report worst-subgroup performance alongside cohort means.

## External validity

This framework has not been run on any real cohort. It is not known how the encoder behaves under sensor drift, firmware changes, or changes in passive-sensing app permissions. It is not known how the dynamics behave under participant context changes (a move, a job change, a season change). It is not known whether the regime labels recovered on real cohorts will align with clinically meaningful states. These are exactly the questions that need to be answered before this framework is useful for science, let alone for any clinical context.
